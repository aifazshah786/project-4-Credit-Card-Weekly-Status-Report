.# project-4- Credit Card Analytics Dashboard (Power BI)

##   Project Overview

- Built a two-page interactive Power BI dashboard  
- Analyzed credit card customer behavior and transaction performance  
- Focused on revenue trends, customer segmentation, and weekly analysis  
- Enabled dynamic filtering using slicers (Week, Gender, Income Group)

---

##   Business Objectives

- Identify high-value customer segments  
- Track weekly transaction trends  
- Analyze revenue distribution by category  
- Compare income group performance  
- Evaluate customer spending behavior  

---

##  Key KPIs

- Total Revenue: 57M  
- Total Customers: 10K  
- Total Transaction Amount: 46M  
- Total Interest Earned: 7.98M  
- Total Transaction Volume: 667K  
- Average Credit Utilization: 0.27  

---

##  Dashboard 1: Customer Analysis

- Revenue by Income Group  
  - High income group generates maximum revenue  
  - Medium income shows stable contribution  
  - Low income contributes least  

- Revenue by Age Group  
  - 46–60 age group is top contributor  
  - 36–45 is second highest segment  

- Gender-Based Analysis  
  - Balanced distribution across segments  

- Category Contribution  
  - Bills and Entertainment are top revenue drivers  
  - Travel shows lowest contribution  

---

##  Dashboard 2: Transaction Analysis

- Weekly Transaction Trend  
  - Identified fluctuations across months  
  - Detected peak revenue weeks  

- Revenue % by Category  
  - Bills highest contributor  
  - Entertainment and Fuel follow  

- Spending Behavior  
  - Some categories generate higher revenue with fewer transactions  

- Weekly Growth Tracking  
  - Analyzed performance changes week-over-week  

---

##   Challenges Faced

- Week sorting issue (Week 1, 10, 11 problem)  
- Creating Serial Week column for proper order  
- Ensuring slicers control entire dashboard  
- Managing DAX growth calculations  
- Designing clean and professional layout  

---

##   Tools & Technologies Used

- Power BI Desktop  
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)  
- Data Modeling  
- Interactive Slicers  
- KPI Cards  
- Time Intelligence Functions  

---

##  Business Recommendations

- Focus marketing on high-income segment  
- Create loyalty offers for 46–60 age group  
- Improve low-performing categories like Travel  
- Run targeted campaigns during low transaction weeks  
- Introduce cashback strategies for mid-income customers  

---


## Overall Conclusion

### 🔹 Dashboard 1 – Customer Analysis Conclusion

- High-income customers are the primary revenue drivers.
- Customers aged 46–60 contribute the highest revenue share.
- Bills and Entertainment categories dominate total revenue.
- Gender distribution is relatively balanced, indicating broad customer engagement.
- Mid-income segment presents strong growth opportunity with targeted offers.

# Business Impact:
This dashboard helps management identify high-value customer segments and focus marketing strategies on profitable demographics.

---

### 🔹 Dashboard 2 – Transaction Analysis Conclusion

- Weekly transaction trends show noticeable fluctuations across months.
- Certain weeks demonstrate revenue spikes, indicating seasonal or behavioral patterns.
- Bills category consistently generates the highest revenue contribution.
- Some categories generate higher revenue despite lower transaction volumes.
- Weekly growth analysis helps monitor performance momentum.

## Business Impact:
This dashboard enables performance tracking at weekly level, helping businesses take timely decisions to optimize campaigns, offers, and customer engagement strategies.

---

##  Final Takeaway

This project demonstrates how interactive Power BI dashboards can transform raw transaction data into actionable business insights.  

By combining:
- Customer segmentation  
- Revenue analysis  
- Weekly trend monitoring  
- Category performance tracking  

The dashboard provides a complete 360° analytical view of credit card business performance.
